Best Cafés in Koh Samui

Specialty espresso, fresh coconut lattes, sea-view terraces and jungle garden hideaways — the island's best coffee stops, honestly reviewed.

Home › Restaurants › Best Cafés in Koh Samui
Cafés Updated January 2025 6 min read

Koh Samui's café scene has changed beyond recognition in the last five years. What was once limited to Nescafé sachets and sweet Thai iced coffee from a roadside cart now includes a genuine specialty scene — single-origin beans, proper espresso technique, third-wave roasters — sitting comfortably alongside the old-school island favourites that have been here for decades.

The best cafés on the island tend to cluster around Bophut and Fisherman's Village in the north, with a handful of standouts scattered across Chaweng and the quieter south. Here are the ones actually worth going out of your way for.

The Best Cafés in Koh Samui

01
☕ Specialty Coffee 📍 Chaweng Best for: Serious coffee lovers

Ristr8to Lab

The best cup of coffee on the island, full stop. Ristr8to Lab is the Koh Samui outpost of the well-regarded Chiang Mai specialty coffee brand, and the quality has made the journey south intact. The baristas know what they are doing — extraction times are dialled in, the milk work is precise, and they will happily talk you through the origin of what is in your cup if you are interested.

The space is clean and modern with enough seating to settle in for a few hours, reliable wifi, and air conditioning that actually works. If you are a coffee snob — and there is no shame in that — this is your spot. Order the flat white or ask what single-origin they are pulling that week.

Opens: 8am  ·  Price range: 100–180 THB  ·  Wifi: Yes

02
🥐 Breakfast & Brunch 📍 Bophut Best for: Morning coffee + food

Bar Baguette

One of the most consistently good café experiences on the island. Bar Baguette sits in Bophut with a terrace that catches the morning breeze, and it does the simple things well — fresh pastries, good coffee, a breakfast menu that goes beyond the standard tourist offering. The egg dishes are excellent, the baguettes live up to the name, and the coffee is a cut above most places in the area.

This is the kind of place you end up coming back to every morning without really planning to. The crowd is a mix of expats, long-stay visitors, and locals — always a good sign. Go early on weekends as it fills up by 9am and the terrace tables are worth waiting for.

Opens: 7:30am  ·  Price range: 120–280 THB  ·  Wifi: Yes

03
🌅 Beach Café 📍 Bophut — Fisherman's Village Best for: Sunset drinks

Coco Tam's

Technically it crosses into beach bar territory by the evening, but Coco Tam's earns its place on this list for the afternoon coffee experience alone. The swing seats over the water are as good as they look in every photo, the iced drinks are well made, and the setting — right on the Fisherman's Village beachfront — is genuinely hard to beat.

Come in the mid-afternoon, order an iced coconut coffee or a cold brew, and claim a swing seat before they fill up. The crowd thickens as the sun drops and the fire show starts — which is also worth staying for if your evening has no other plans. One of those places that feels like Koh Samui at its best.

Opens: 11am  ·  Price range: 100–220 THB  ·  Wifi: Yes

04
🌿 Organic 📍 South Samui Best for: Healthy breakfast

Sweet Sisters Café

Down in the quieter south of the island, Sweet Sisters is one of those places that rewards the effort of finding it. The focus here is organic, plant-forward food with genuinely good coffee alongside — a combination that sounds like it should be worthy and dull, but is neither. The coconut cake is exceptional, the green curry is among the best on the island, and the coffee is sourced and made with proper care.

The atmosphere is relaxed and unhurried in a way that reflects the south of Samui generally — fewer tourists, more space, a slower pace. There is also a small shop selling local honey, baked goods, and Thai handicrafts. Worth building a morning around, especially if you are staying on the south coast.

Opens: 8am  ·  Price range: 80–200 THB  ·  Wifi: Yes

Also Worth Knowing About

Three more cafés that consistently come up in recommendations — no photos yet, but all confirmed real and worth your time.

Roastery Village — Fisherman's Village, Bophut

A specialty roastery café tucked inside Fisherman's Village that takes its coffee seriously. The owner roasts on site and will walk you through the options — light, medium, or dark roast, and which origin suits your preference. Spacious enough to work in for a few hours, with a relaxed atmosphere that does not rush you out. A strong pick for anyone who wants to understand what is in their cup rather than just drink it.

📍 Fisherman's Village, Bophut  ·  Best for: Specialty coffee and remote work

Boy's Organic Coffee — Central Samui

A genuinely rare thing: a café growing its own beans on the island. Khun Boy cultivates Arabica on his small plot and serves the results in a rustic, open-air space surrounded by palms and banana plants. The coffee is earthy, robust, and nothing like the international chain options available elsewhere. Ask for the signature coconut coffee — espresso over coconut water and ice — which sounds unusual and works entirely.

📍 Central Samui  ·  Best for: Local beans, unique experience

The Coffee Club — Chaweng Beach

An Australian chain, yes — but one that has earned its following in Koh Samui for good reason. The location facing Chaweng Beach is excellent, the wifi is reliable, the menu is extensive, and the air conditioning is a genuine asset on hot afternoons. Not where you go for the most interesting cup on the island, but very much where you go when you need a comfortable, consistent, entirely competent coffee experience. Multiple locations across the island including Central Festival mall.

📍 Multiple locations — Chaweng Beach, Central Festival  ·  Best for: Reliability, wifi, air-con

💡

Insider Tip: Thai iced coffee from a street vendor — strong local robusta with condensed milk poured over ice — is still one of the best value drinks on the island at 30–50 THB. Worth trying alongside the specialty options, not instead of them.

What Does Coffee Cost in Koh Samui?

Street vendor: 30–50 THB for Thai iced coffee — strong, sweet, very good.

Local café: 60–100 THB for an iced Americano or cappuccino.

Specialty café: 100–180 THB for a flat white or single-origin filter.

Hotel café: 150–250 THB for the exact same drink in a nicer cup.
