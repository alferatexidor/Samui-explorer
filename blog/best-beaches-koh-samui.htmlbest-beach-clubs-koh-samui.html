Best Beach Clubs in Koh Samui

Sunbeds, cocktails, DJs, and ocean views — the complete guide to Koh Samui's beach club scene.

Home› Beach Clubs› Best Beach Clubs in Koh Samui
Beach Clubs Updated January 2025 6 min read

Koh Samui's beach club scene has grown considerably in the last decade. What was once a handful of basic beach bars has evolved into a mix of internationally recognised brands and genuinely good locally-owned venues. You can spend a whole day at the right beach club and not want to leave — drinks, food, music, and the sea all in one place.

This guide covers the best of them — including what you will actually pay, when to go, and who each one suits.

Top Beach Clubs in Koh Samui

01
Most Famous

Nikki Beach — Lipa Noi

The international name that put Koh Samui's beach club scene on the map. Set on the quieter west coast, Nikki Beach is all white, all day, all beautiful. Sunday brunches here have become legendary — full of groups celebrating something, dancing on the furniture, and spending more than they planned to. A minimum spend applies (typically 2,000–3,000 THB per person at weekends). Go on a Sunday, make a day of it, and plan your Monday to be very quiet.

02
Best Value

Ark Bar — Chaweng Beach

On the central stretch of Chaweng Beach, Ark Bar is the most reliably busy beach club on the island. Less pretentious than Nikki Beach, no minimum spend, daily pool parties from around 2pm. This is where you come if you want to meet other travellers, dance in the afternoon, and drink something cold. Not for those seeking a quiet sunbed — that is not the point here.

03
Sunset Views

Beach Republic — Lamai

One of the more design-conscious venues on the island. A cluster of elevated daybeds, a good pool, consistent music programming, and a restaurant that holds its own in the evenings. Lamai is slightly less hectic than Chaweng, which means Beach Republic tends to attract a slightly older crowd who want quality over noise. Highly recommended for a special afternoon.

04

CocoTam's — Bophut

A local favourite rather than a full-scale beach club. Bar stools and hammocks on the sand, a friendly local crowd, and a sound system that plays everything except what you expect. Not fancy, genuinely charming. Come for a sundowner and stay for dinner at one of the restaurants nearby in Fisherman's Village.

05

Jungle Club — Chaweng Hills

Strictly speaking not a beach club — but worth including because it offers something none of the beach-level venues can: a panoramic view across Chaweng Bay from a hillside pool. The vibe is more bohemian, the cocktails are good, and the sunset from up here is something else. The road up is steep so you need a confident driver or a local taxi.

What to Expect at a Koh Samui Beach Club

Prices and Minimum Spend

Most beach clubs in Koh Samui work on a minimum spend model rather than a flat entry fee. This typically ranges from 500–3,000 THB depending on the day and the venue. Weekend rates are almost always higher. Check the website or call ahead before you show up — minimum spends have increased across most venues in the last few years and are often not prominently advertised.

Best Time to Go

Arrive early (by 11am) to secure good sunbeds — at the better venues these are gone by noon at weekends. The most energetic period at most clubs is 2–5pm, with live DJs and maximum activity. If you want the atmosphere but fewer crowds, Sunday mornings after a big Saturday event are often surprisingly good value and atmosphere.

💡

Insider Tip: Booking a sunbed or daybed in advance (usually possible online) secures your spot and often comes with a food and drink credit included. It costs the same but means you are not hovering near the entrance waiting for something to open up.

Beach Clubs by Location

Chaweng: Most choice, most energy, always busy. Ark Bar, The Kudo Beach Club, and several smaller venues along the beach.

Lamai: Better design, slightly less crowded, better food. Beach Republic and a handful of smaller independent venues.

Lipa Noi (west coast): Nikki Beach. The sunset views here are the best on the island — west-facing means you are looking straight into the sun going down.

Bophut: Lower key, more local, better food nearby. CocoTam's and a few other beach bars rather than full clubs.
